# 50. 鼎 Dǐng — The Cauldron

**Korean** — 화풍정 (火風鼎)
**Trigrams** — Fire (離) over Wind (巽)
**Binary (bottom→top)** — `011101`

## Judgment
The cauldron. Supreme good fortune. Success.

## Image
Fire over wood — the noble one consolidates their fate by making their position correct.

## Interpretation
Following Revolution — the new culture, the new form. Sacred container for transformation. What you cook in this vessel feeds many.

## Changing line notes
- **Line 1** — A cauldron with legs upturned. Furthers removal of stagnating stuff. One takes a concubine for the sake of her son. No blame.
- **Line 2** — There is food in the cauldron. My comrades are envious, but they cannot harm me. Good fortune.
- **Line 3** — The handle of the cauldron is altered. One is impeded in their way of life. The fat of the pheasant is not eaten. Once rain falls, remorse is spent. Good fortune comes in the end.
- **Line 4** — The legs of the cauldron are broken. The prince's meal is spilled and his person soiled. Misfortune.
- **Line 5** — The cauldron has yellow handles, golden carrying rings. Perseverance furthers.
- **Line 6** — The cauldron has rings of jade. Great good fortune. Nothing that would not act to further.
