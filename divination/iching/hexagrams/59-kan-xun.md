# 59. 渙 Huàn — Dispersion / Dissolving

**Korean** — 풍수환 (風水渙)
**Trigrams** — Wind (巽) over Water (坎)
**Binary (bottom→top)** — `010011`

## Judgment
Dispersion. Success. The king approaches their temple. It furthers one to cross great water. Perseverance furthers.

## Image
Wind drives over the water — kings of old sacrificed to the Lord and built temples.

## Interpretation
Frozen things melt — emotional armor, factionalism, calcified resentments. Ceremony helps. Once it disperses, what's real underneath is recoverable.

## Changing line notes
- **Line 1** — He brings help with the strength of a horse. Good fortune.
- **Line 2** — At the dissolution he hurries to that which supports him. Remorse disappears.
- **Line 3** — He dissolves their self. No remorse.
- **Line 4** — He dissolves their bond with their group. Supreme good fortune. Dispersion leads in turn to accumulation. This is something that ordinary people do not think of.
- **Line 5** — His loud cries are as dissolving as sweat. Dissolution! A king abides without blame.
- **Line 6** — He dissolves their blood. Departing, keeping at a distance, going out, is without blame.
