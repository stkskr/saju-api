# 21. 噬嗑 Shì Hé — Biting Through

**Korean** — 화뢰서합 (火雷噬嗑)
**Trigrams** — Fire (離) over Thunder (震)
**Binary (bottom→top)** — `100101`

## Judgment
Biting through has success. It furthers one to let justice be administered.

## Image
Thunder and lightning — kings of old made firm the laws through clearly defined penalties.

## Interpretation
Something is jammed; chew through it. Decisive action with clarity. Not violence — justice with teeth.

## Changing line notes
- **Line 1** — Feet in stocks so the toes disappear — no blame.
- **Line 2** — Bites through tender meat so the nose disappears — no blame.
- **Line 3** — Bites on old dried meat and strikes on something poisonous — slight humiliation, no blame.
- **Line 4** — Bites on dried gristly meat — receives metal arrows. Useful to be mindful of difficulties.
- **Line 5** — Bites on dried lean meat — receives yellow gold. Perseverance and awareness of danger — no blame.
- **Line 6** — Wears a cangue, so his ears disappear — misfortune.
