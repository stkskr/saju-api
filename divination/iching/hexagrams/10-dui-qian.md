# 10. 履 Lǚ — Treading / Conduct

**Korean** — 천택리 (天澤履)
**Trigrams** — Heaven (乾) over Lake (兌)
**Binary (bottom→top)** — `110111`

## Judgment
Treading on the tiger's tail — it does not bite. Success.

## Image
Heaven above, lake below — the noble one discriminates high and low, settling the people's purpose.

## Interpretation
Walking through a dangerous situation with grace. Manners, calm, and attentive presence keep you safe where force would get you bitten.

## Changing line notes
- **Line 1** — Simple conduct — progress without blame.
- **Line 2** — Treading a smooth, level path — quiet person, good fortune.
- **Line 3** — A one-eyed man can still see; a lame man can still walk — but treading on the tiger's tail, he is bitten.
- **Line 4** — Treading on the tiger's tail — caution and good fortune.
- **Line 5** — Resolute conduct — perseverance with awareness of danger.
- **Line 6** — Look at the conduct; weigh the omens. When the cycle is complete, supreme good fortune.
