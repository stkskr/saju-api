# 34. 大壯 Dà Zhuàng — Great Power

**Korean** — 뇌천대장 (雷天大壯)
**Trigrams** — Thunder (震) over Heaven (乾)
**Binary (bottom→top)** — `111100`

## Judgment
Great power. Perseverance furthers.

## Image
Thunder in heaven above — the noble one does not tread upon paths that do not accord with established order.

## Interpretation
You have the power. The test is whether you use it for what's right or just because you can. Strength without righteousness becomes a wrecking ball.

## Changing line notes
- **Line 1** — Power in the toes. Continuing brings misfortune. This is undoubtedly true.
- **Line 2** — Perseverance brings good fortune.
- **Line 3** — The inferior person works through power; the noble one does not act thus. To continue is dangerous. A goat butts against a hedge and gets its horns entangled.
- **Line 4** — Perseverance brings good fortune. Remorse disappears. The hedge opens — no entanglement. Power depends upon the axle of a big cart.
- **Line 5** — Loses the goat with ease — no remorse.
- **Line 6** — A goat butts against a hedge. It cannot go back, cannot go forward. Nothing serves to further. But if you note the difficulty, this brings good fortune.
