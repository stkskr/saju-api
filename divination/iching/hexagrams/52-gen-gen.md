# 52. 艮 Gèn — Keeping Still / Mountain

**Korean** — 중산간 (重山艮)
**Trigrams** — Mountain (艮) over Mountain (艮)
**Binary (bottom→top)** — `001001`

## Judgment
Keeping still. Keeping their back still so they no longer feel their body. They go into their courtyard and do not see the people. No blame.

## Image
Mountains standing close together — the noble one does not permit their thoughts to go beyond their situation.

## Interpretation
Sit. Stop. Be a mountain. The mind that won't stop is the mind that can't see clearly. Discipline of stillness — radical, simple, hard.

## Changing line notes
- **Line 1** — Keeping their toes still. No blame. Continued perseverance furthers.
- **Line 2** — Keeping their calves still. They cannot rescue him whom they follow. Their heart is not glad.
- **Line 3** — Keeping their hips still. Making their sacrum stiff. Dangerous. The heart suffocates.
- **Line 4** — Keeping their trunk still. No blame.
- **Line 5** — Keeping their jaws still. The words have order. Remorse disappears.
- **Line 6** — Noblehearted keeping still. Good fortune.
