# Branch six-combinations (지지육합 / 地支六合)

Six paired branches that bond, sometimes producing a transformed element when surrounding chart supports.

| Branch A | Branch B | Korean | Transforms to | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 子 | 丑 | 자축 | earth (weak) | Often a quiet, conserving combination. Earth element if surrounding chart supports; otherwise just bonding. |
| 寅 | 亥 | 인해 | wood | Strong wood-producing combination — water nourishes wood. |
| 卯 | 戌 | 묘술 | fire | A famous combo — wood meets dry earth and ignites fire (when conditions support). |
| 辰 | 酉 | 진유 | metal | Earth generates metal — solid combo. |
| 巳 | 申 | 사신 | water | Notoriously unstable — also a punishment pair. Combines AND fights. |
| 午 | 未 | 오미 | no transform (sun-moon) | The Sun and Moon combination. Bonding without a clean element transform; sometimes referenced as fire-leaning. |

## Reading notes

- A six-combination involving the Day Branch and the Spouse Palace (day branch) suggests close marital bonding.
- 巳申 is special — it's *simultaneously* a combination, a punishment, and a break. Three relationships in one pair create complicated dynamics.
- 寅亥 is *simultaneously* a combination and a break — bonding with breakdown built in.
