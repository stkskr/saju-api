# Branch punishments (형 / 刑)

Punishments are friction relationships that don't fit cleanly into combinations or clashes. Four types.

## 지세지형 — Punishment of Power

**Branches** — 寅 · 巳 · 申

Three-pillar punishment of wielding power. When all three branches appear, indicates someone who uses force, authority, or influence — for good or ill. Friction even among allies.

## 무은지형 — Punishment of Ingratitude

**Branches** — 丑 · 戌 · 未

Three-pillar punishment of ingratitude. Earth punishing earth — broken trust within family or close ties, betrayal by those who owed loyalty.

## 무례지형 — Punishment of Rudeness

**Branches** — 子 · 卯

Two-branch mutual punishment — rude, disrespectful behavior, lack of social grace.

## 자형 — Self-Punishment

**Branches** — 辰-辰 · 午-午 · 酉-酉 · 亥-亥

Same-branch pairs (辰辰, 午午, 酉酉, 亥亥). Self-sabotage, repeating one's own mistakes, internal conflict.

## Reading notes

- 寅巳申 contains a triple relationship: punishment + 巳申 combination + clashes (寅申, 巳寅 indirectly). Volatile.
- 丑戌未 includes the 戌未 break and the 丑戌 (earth grinding) tension. Family conflicts a common manifestation.
- 子卯 is also a clash partner for other branches, but specifically when together they're labeled punishment of rudeness.
- Self-punishment (자형) is read as repeating one's own patterns — psychological self-sabotage.
