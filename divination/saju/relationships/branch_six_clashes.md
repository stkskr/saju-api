# Branch six-clashes (지지육충 / 六沖)

Branches six positions apart clash directly. These produce visible conflict, transition, instability — but also energy and motion.

| Branch A | Branch B | Korean | Dynamic |
|---|---|---|---|
| 子 | 午 | 자오 | water vs fire — north vs south, peak vs peak |
| 丑 | 未 | 축미 | winter earth vs summer earth — also called 'storage clash' |
| 寅 | 申 | 인신 | wood vs metal — also a punishment pair |
| 卯 | 酉 | 묘유 | wood vs metal — east-west axis, fierce clash |
| 辰 | 戌 | 진술 | earth vs earth — 'storage clash' between water-grave and fire-grave |
| 巳 | 亥 | 사해 | fire vs water — opposite seasons, fire's peak vs water's start |

## Reading notes

- A clash isn't automatically bad — it can release stuck energy, force needed change, or open doors that were closed.
- Day-Hour clash: friction with children or in late life.
- Year-Month clash: tension with parents/ancestral environment in youth.
- Day-Month clash: career or family-home conflict.
- 辰戌丑未 (the four earth branches) clashing each other is called 'storage clash' — opens up the buried stems inside each branch, releasing their energies, often disruptively.
