## About nayin (납음/納音)

Each pair of consecutive jiazi shares one of 30 nayin 'sound elements.' This is a *secondary* element layer separate from the stem and branch elements — used in compatibility readings and older bazi schools.

## About empty branches (공망/空亡)

Each set of 10 jiazi (a 旬 / 순) leaves two branches without a stem partner. Those two are the 'empty' branches for any pillar in that group. Empty branches in a chart indicate areas of life that feel hollow, unstable, or detached — not necessarily bad, but unanchored.

See `nayin.md` for nayin element descriptions and `empty_branches.md` for the full 공망 lookup pattern.
