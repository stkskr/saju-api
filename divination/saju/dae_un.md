# Dae-un (대운/大運) — 10-year luck cycle

The 대운 is the sequence of 10-year periods that color a person's life. Each 대운 is a stem-branch pillar that succeeds the next, derived from the Month Pillar.

## How direction is determined

Direction depends on the **gender of the year stem polarity**:

| Year stem polarity | Person's sex | Dae-un direction |
|--------------------|--------------|------------------|
| Yang (甲丙戊庚壬)  | Male         | Forward (順行)   |
| Yang               | Female       | Backward (逆行)  |
| Yin (乙丁己辛癸)   | Male         | Backward (逆行)  |
| Yin                | Female       | Forward (順行)   |

The 대운 pillars are then read forward or backward from the Month Pillar through the 60-jiazi cycle.

## How starting age is determined

Count the days between the birth time and the *nearest applicable solar term* (next term if forward direction; previous term if backward direction). Divide that number of days by 3 — the result is the age (in years) at which the first 대운 begins.

The exact formula:
- Days between birth and reference solar term, ÷ 3 = starting age in years.
- A remainder of 1 day ≈ 4 months; 2 days ≈ 8 months.

## How to read a dae-un

Each 대운 is read as a pillar layered on top of the natal chart:
1. The 대운 stem interacts with the natal stems (combinations, clashes).
2. The 대운 branch interacts with the natal branches (combinations, trinities, clashes, harms, punishments).
3. The 대운 introduces or amplifies certain Ten Gods.
4. Compare the 대운's element to the 용신 — supportive or opposing?

A 대운 that brings the 용신 element supports the chart for a decade. One that brings the opposing element creates a difficult decade.

## Mid-cycle transitions

The first 5 years of a 대운 are dominated by the *stem*; the second 5 years are dominated by the *branch*. A 대운 may shift dramatically at the 5-year mark.

## Practical reading tip

Map out the next 3-4 대운 from the person's current age. Look for major transitions — clash, trinity completion, sudden element shift — and treat those as pivot points in life narrative.
